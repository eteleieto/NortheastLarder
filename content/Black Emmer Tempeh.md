## Background

This experiment involved [[Blackening (Enzymatic)|blackening]] [[Emmer Tempeh]] over the course of a month at 140°F, a process similar to making [[Black Garlic|black garlic]].

## Instructions

1.  **Blackening**  
    Place the tempeh in a dehydrator or proofer set to 140°F. Leave for one month until fully blackened.

## Notes and Observations

- When removed, the result had the taste of raisins and dark chocolate, with a soft, raisin-like texture.
- Unlike [[Blackened Soybean Tempeh|blackened soybean tempeh]], there was hardly any mushroomy taste; it was almost entirely sweet.
- Interesting applications may include a range of desserts, but also savory applications like [[Mole|mole]]. It could fill a similar place in a pantry to chocolate.
- A portion was later dried, ground with sugar, and made into a blackened tempeh sugar. The taste is unique—fruity and chocolatey but altogether hard to describe.

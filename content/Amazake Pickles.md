## Background

This experiment uses [[Amazake Project|amazake]] as a sweetener for a pickling liquid, creating an alternative to traditional sugar-based brines. The pickling liquid is a simple mixture of [[Distilled White Vinegar|distilled white vinegar]], amazake, and salt, which will be used to pickle vegetables.

### Ingredients 

4 parts Distilled White Vinegar

1 part Amazake

Salt (to taste)

Vegetables (unspecified)

--- column-break ---

### Equipment

Glass Jar

Kitchen Scale

Blender

--- end-multi-column

## Instructions

1.  **Prepare Liquid**  
    Combine the distilled white vinegar, amazake, and salt. Blend until the liquid is smooth and homogenous.

2.  **Prepare Vegetables**  
    Wash and cut the vegetables as desired.

3.  **Combine**  
    Place the prepared vegetables into a glass jar and pour the pickling liquid over them, ensuring they are fully submerged.

4.  **Store**  
    Seal the jar and store in the refrigerator.

## Notes and Observations

- The amazake used for the pickling liquid was clarified and then blended to create a smooth consistency.
- No additional spices or seasonings were added to this batch. This was done to create a baseline flavor profile, but they could be incorporated in future versions.
